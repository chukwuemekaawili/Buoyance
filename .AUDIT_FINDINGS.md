## Findings Summary

### ✅ FIXED IN THIS SESSION

**1. Rent Relief Formula (taxEngine.ts)**
- **Old logic:** MIN(Rent Paid, ₦200,000, 1/6 of gross)
- **NTA 2025 law:** 20% of annual rent paid, capped at ₦500,000
- **Fixed:** Updated `calculateRentRelief()` and `RENT_RELIEF` constant
- **Impact:** Every PAYE calculation now produces correct rent relief

**2. PAYE Minimum Tax Floor (taxEngine.ts & payrollEngine.ts)**
- **Rule:** Where reliefs reduce tax below 1% of gross, the floor is 1% of gross income
- **Status:** Implemented in `calculateGlobalPIT()` with dual logic:
  - If taxable income ≤ 0: return 1% of gross
  - If calculated tax < 1% of gross: return 1% of gross
- **Note:** This rule is marked **provisionally valid** (PwC cites it; not yet pinned to NTA 2025 gazette section). Keep this assumption but plan to verify directly against the signed Act before final practitioner sign-off.

---

### ⚠️ DEFINITE PRE-LAUNCH FIX REQUIRED (DB Only)

**3. CIT Small Company Threshold (Supabase `tax_rules` table)**
- **Current in DB:** `small_company_threshold_kobo: 10000000000` (₦100M) ❌
- **NTA 2025 law:** ₦50M turnover AND ₦250M fixed assets (excluding professional services)
- **Required action:** Manually edit Supabase `tax_rules` → CIT row:
  - Change `small_company_threshold_kobo` from `10000000000` to `5000000000`
  - Update `description` field to reflect ≤₦50M threshold
- **Why:** The signed Act uses ₦50M, not ₦100M. Earlier drafts said ₦100M; the final signed version changed this.
- **Impact:** Small businesses under ₦50M are currently being over-classified as large companies and charged 30% CIT instead of 0%

---

### 🟡 HIGH-PRIORITY NEXT (Not a launch blocker, but material)

**4. VAT Zero-Rating & Exemption Enforcement**
- **Current state:** VAT rates table in DB includes `exempt_categories: ["basic food items", "medical services", "educational services", "exported goods"]`
- **Problem:** The VAT calculator applies 7.5% flat; it does not enforce these exemptions
- **Risk:** Pharmacy, food wholesale, healthcare, and education sectors will over-remit VAT
- **Solution:** Add transaction-type field to VAT inputs (standard/zero-rated/exempt) and apply conditional rates
- **Timeline:** Include in first update if targeting these sectors in initial launch; otherwise defer post-launch

---

### 🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY NEXT

**5. Part-Year Employment (PAYE)**
- **Issue:** PAYE calculation annualizes mid-year hires (multiply monthly by 12) without adjusting for hire date
- **Example:** Employee hired July 1, monthly gross ₦500K → annual is calculated as ₦6M (wrong: should be ₦3M for 6-month year)
- **Fix:** Add `hire_date` to payroll input; calculate annualized income only for months worked
- **Timeline:** First update

**6. Benefit-in-Kind (BIK) Income**
- **Issue:** Payroll engine only handles cash salary components
- **NTA 2025:** Company housing, vehicles, and other non-cash benefits are taxable income
- **Current impact:** Employees with company cars/housing are under-taxed
- **Fix:** Add BIK line items to payroll structure with valuation rules
- **Timeline:** First update
